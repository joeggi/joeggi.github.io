Games Portfolio

This section displays my computer game development experience, including games I've developed using Unity/C#.

Corrupt Eldritch Order

Corrupt Eldritch Order is a single-player, 2D-action platformer game. My team aimed to create an ultra polished game this semester, and so we took inspiration from GunStar Heroes, a simpler case study compared to some of our other projects.

As QA Director, I was responsible for fixing bugs in essentially every area of the game. However, my work specialized in implementing and testing player mobility, aiming, bullet and weapon interactions, and environment interactions.

  • Corrupt Eldritch Order

Tails of War

Tails of War is a turn-based tactics game with online multiplayer and singleplayer options. I'm really happy with the theme we chose for this game, which includes 6 different cat characters that battle it out on various map layouts.

AS QA Director, I was in control of quality in all aspects of the game, but focused much of my work on the battle system, ensuring seamless transitions between player turns. This meant thorough testing of edge cases for player actions.

  • Tails of War

Cellosseum

Cellosseum was my first project with WolverineSoft. This game is a bullet hell case study of Nova Drift, set in a faraway planet where a lone cell is fighting to start new life.

My work on Cellosseum included thorough programming and testing of enemies, including rigorous mathematical programming of projectiles, enemy rotations and movement speeds, as well as enemy pathfinding.

  • Cellosseum

Carrots In Space

Carrots in Space was the final project for my first game development class at U of M. I teamed up with 4 other students to make this game in only a couple months.

My work on Carrots in Space included basic animation implementation, theme design, cutscene design, and guidance design. My technical contributions included programming many of the physics interactions and art integrations. I also recorded and edited the trailer for the game, which can be found below.

  • Play at itch.io!
  • Trailer

Speed Dating

Speed Dating my first ever independent game development project for my game development class. In this fast paced game, the player must drive quickly and carefully, while still paying attention to dialogue from their date in the car with them. This game is pretty technically rough around the edges, but I was proud of the design and theme I created.

This is a reimagined version of Zelda for the NES, made by myself and my teammate. My work included thorough programming of the environment as well as enemy behaviors, item drops and item effects, and the UI systems.
